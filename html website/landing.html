What is AVAA?

A robot that cares as much as you do

AVAA is a Turtlebot3 Waffle Pi powered by ROS2 Humble. Using YOLOv8 computer vision and LiDAR, it detects and follows your child autonomously — keeping a safe distance, avoiding obstacles, and streaming live video to your phone or laptop.

Built on a dual-Pi architecture — the Pi 4B handles navigation and following, while a dedicated Pi 5 runs a full AI companion: voice conversations, interactive games, hazard detection, and story time.

Everything AVAA can do

Autonomous Following

AVAA locks onto the nearest person using DeepSORT tracking and follows them through rooms, adjusting speed and direction in real time.

Live Camera Feed

Stream the robot's camera view directly to any browser on the same WiFi. See exactly what AVAA sees, with YOLO detection overlaid.

Emergency Stop

One tap halts all robot movement instantly. The robot continuously publishes zero velocity until manually resumed.

Pi 5 · AI
AI Voice Companion

AVAA talks to your child — answers questions, tells stories, and holds real conversations using an AI running locally on the Pi 5.

Pi 5 · Interactive
Scavenger Hunt

AVAA challenges the child — "find something red!" — and uses its camera to verify when they bring the object back. Celebrates with them when they succeed.

Pi 5 · AI
Hazard Detection

AI vision detects dangerous situations — like a child holding a sharp object — and immediately alerts parents before anything happens.

Gesture Control

The child can control AVAA with natural hand gestures — show an open palm to pause following, thumbs up to resume. MediaPipe detects gestures in real time via the robot camera.

Auto Home Docking

When the child is lost for 2 minutes or the parent presses Home, AVAA enters ArUco marker mode — scanning for its home marker and autonomously docking to its base position.

Remote Joystick

Take direct control via the built-in virtual joystick. Drive AVAA anywhere from your phone or laptop with speed control and live velocity readout.

SLAM Mapping

Using Google Cartographer, AVAA builds a precise map of your home. Combine with Nav2 for autonomous room-to-room navigation.

Pi 5 · Coming soon
Hide & Seek

Future mode where AVAA actively searches the house for the child, using its map and AI vision to track them down — a fully autonomous game of hide and seek.

Under the hood

How AVAA works

01
Camera captures the scene

The USB webcam streams raw frames at 640×480. These are served via MJPEG from the Pi's Flask server to any connected device on the network.

02
YOLO runs on the laptop

A connected laptop reads the stream and runs YOLOv8 with DeepSORT tracking. The person angle is calculated and sent back to the Pi via HTTP — offloading heavy inference away from the Pi CPU.

03
LiDAR measures distance

The LDS-02 LiDAR continuously scans the front arc. The follower node reads /scan to determine proximity and decides whether to advance, hold position, or reverse.

04
Commands drive the wheels

The follower publishes Twist messages to /cmd_vel. The OpenCR board translates these into precise motor commands — smooth, responsive, and safe.
